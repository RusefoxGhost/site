GALLERIA AT ROSEVILLE - THE KIDS GALLERY
PLACER COUNTY PARTNERS


Roseville Arts Center - The Roseville Arts Center was founded in 1963 and incorporated as Roseville Community Projects, Inc. in 1966. It has earned a reputation for providing quality arts opportunities to the community as well as restoring and developing property to house these opportunities. Located at the historic Haman House, the Arts Center is currently working to renovate the historic Tower Theater. The Arts Center provides a wonderful opportunity for area children to learn about and personally experience the arts. Roseville Arts Center also partners with several City and Chamber of Commerce programs such as the Roseville Parks and Recreation Arts Programs and the Chamber of Commerce's Arts in Public Places programs. The Arts Center has established and manages a partnership with area schools to provide tours of both the Haman House and Tower Theater for students. Funds from The Kids Gallery will support and expand art education programs for children.


United Way - The United Way California Capital Region was established in the fall of 1923 as the Community Chest and, that year, raised just over $217,000. By 1998, the organization generated almost $20 million and has evolved into the premier health and human service program provider in the area. They are recognized as the number one non-governmental funder of health and human services programs. United Way's Placer County Office was established in 1992, and since that time, local contributions to United Way have increased by 85 percent, while new partnerships have been forged to benefit the citizens of Placer County. Funds from The Kids Gallery will support the following four children's-related agencies:


United Way Long Term Care-Giving for Children Affiliates

  • Big Brothers/Big Sisters "Big Fun" Project - "Big Fun" is a new project designed to provide meaningful life experiences for waiting Little Brothers and Little Sisters from single-parent at-risk families in the Roseville area. Although most children who sign-up to receive a mentor will be matched with a caring adult, a majority of children wait between 4 and 12 months before receiving a Big Brother or Sister. The "Big Fun" project will provide a support system to these children while they are waiting and to demonstrate to them that their community cares about them. In 2000, Big Brothers/Big Sisters hopes to provide "Big Fun" outings children including roller skating parties, trips to Folsom Lake, the movies, ice skating and much more.

  • Caring About Kids - Last year in Placer County, Caring About Kids helped 579 children develop a relationship with a caring adult mentor, and over 200 mentors enrich their own lives through community service. Mentors serving in the Caring About Kids program are encouraged to work side-by-side with children, exposing them to new opportunities. In Roseville alone, mentors have offered hundreds of hours in community service activities (i.e. holiday gift wrapping, children's puppet shows, coat drives, toy drives and sports tournaments) as well as over 1,000 hours of mentoring, including almost 50 hours a month on local gang prevention efforts. Caring About Kids is currently able to place an average of two mentors a day into relationships that last an average of eight months.

United Way Emergency Intervention for Children Affiliates

  • Child Abuse Prevention Council of Placer County - The Child Abuse Prevention Council of Placer County has launched a new project called The Children's Crisis Nursery. Whether temporarily homeless, running from a domestic violence situation, attempting to rid themselves of an addiction, or just needing a respite to calm down, parents sometimes find themselves unable to provide good or even adequate care for their children. This inability often leads to abuse or neglect and the eventual removal of the child from his/her parents. Placer County children who are at-risk of abuse will be given safe refuge at the Nursery for up to 30 days. Most of these children will be under the age of two. The Nursery will also serve as an important link between these at-risk parents and vital educational services to ensure non-abusiveness and healthy self-sufficiency in the future.

  • Placer Women's Center/PEACE for Families - Placer Women's Center/PEACE for Families is a private, non-profit community based organization serving victims of domestic violence. Since 1978, they have operated with the philosophy that every woman, man and child has the basic right to lead a violence-free life. Part of their efforts include a Children's Counseling Program, which has greatly helped develop long-term solutions toward ending violence in Placer County families. Funds from the Children's Tile Wall Project will be allocated to the Children's Counseling Program.
