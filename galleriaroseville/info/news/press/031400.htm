ROSEVILLE ARTS CENTER, UNITED WAY TEAM UP TO MAKE THE CHILDREN'S TILE WALL A REALITY AT GALLERIA AT ROSEVILLE

ROSEVILLE - Urban Shopping Centers, Inc. officials today announced that the United Way/Roseville Arts Center team has been selected to benefit from the Galleria at Roseville's children's tile wall project called "The Kids Gallery." The proceeds from the sale of individual tiles will support several children's programs of United Way and the Roseville Arts Center. The Kids Gallery will become a permanent fixture of the 1.1 million square foot Galleria at Roseville shopping center, which is scheduled to open on August 25, 2000 and be anchored by JCPenney, Macy's, Nordstrom and Sears.

"We are pleased to announce that the United Way/Roseville Arts Center team will help create The Kids Gallery," said Jan Porter of Urban Retail Properties Co., the development, leasing and management affiliate of Urban Shopping Centers, Inc. "Despite a very competitive process with many fine entries, the benefits Placer County residents receive from the Roseville Arts Center and local United Way organizations were compelling. We look forward to working with them on this unique project, which will be a lasting tribute to the youth of Placer County. "

Specifically, the United Way/Roseville Arts Center team will be responsible for selling more than 2200 individual tiles; total project proceeds are estimated to be $100,000. Because of their focus on children's issues, the Roseville Arts Center and four United Way Placer County agencies will benefit from The Kids Gallery:

  • Big Brothers/Big Sisters of Placer County
  • Caring About Kids!
  • Child Abuse Prevention Council of Placer County
  • Placer Women's Center PEACE for Families

"We are thrilled to have the opportunity to partner with Galleria at Roseville to help create The Kids Gallery on behalf of Placer County," said Al Johnson, Roseville City Manager, member of United Way's Placer Leadership team and Chairman of The Kids Gallery Committee. "The completed wall will illustrate the creativity of our community's children, and the funds raised will enable us to provide important services to Placer County families in need."

Galleria at Roseville will host an event during the shopping center's official Grand Opening to unveil The Kids Gallery. Individuals or parents who wish to purchase tiles for their children to decorate should contact the United Way at (916) 774-6124.

"The children's tile wall at Galleria at Roseville will be a source for great pride within the community. Local children will lovingly paint each of the purchased tiles, and the finished wall will vividly illustrate the energy and creativity of the children as well as commemorate the important efforts of United Way and the Roseville Arts Center. We are convinced The Kids Gallery will be a wonderful experience for all of the participants that help make it a reality. Most importantly, the proceeds from the project will be used to benefit the entire community," Porter concluded.

Galleria at Roseville is owned and developed by Urban Shopping Centers, Inc., a self-administered real estate investment trust (REIT), which is in the business of owning, acquiring, managing, leasing, developing and redeveloping super-regional and regional malls. The company opened Brandon TownCenter in Tampa, Florida in February 1995 and Wolfchase Galleria in Memphis, Tennessee in February 1997, a second Tampa-area mall, Citrus Park Town Center, in March 1999, and expects to open Galleria at Roseville on August 25, 2000 and The Streets at Southpoint in Durham, North Carolina spring 2002. Urban Shopping Centers, Inc. owns interests in several of the premier shopping centers in the United States including Oakbrook Center (Oak Brook, Illinois), Century City Shopping Center & Marketplace (Los Angeles, California), Water Tower Place (Chicago, Illinois), Old Orchard Center (Skokie, Illinois), Copley Place (Boston, Massachusetts), San Francisco Shopping Centre (San Francisco, California) and the Galleria (Houston, Texas), as well as in Urban Retail Properties Co., its property management, leasing and development affiliate. Urban Retail Properties Co. is one of the nation's largest retail property managers, managing more than 60 million square feet of space in 26 states and the District of Columbia.
