GALLERIA AT ROSEVILLE ANNOUNCES CHILDREN'S AND JUNIOR'S APPAREL STORES

abercrombie, Children's Place, Anchor Blue, Charlotte Russe, d.e.m.o., Juxtapose and The Buckle to join the Galleria lineup


ROSEVILLE - Urban Shopping Centers, Inc. (NYSE: URB), announced today many popular and new to market children's and junior's apparel stores that will join the 120 store lineup at Galleria at Roseville, a 1.1 million square foot regional shopping center scheduled to open August 25, 2000. Anchored by Nordstrom, Macy's, JCPenney and Sears, the Galleria will offer shoppers a wide variety of children's and junior's apparel stores, as well as a vast selection of footwear; home furnishings; sporting goods and other specialty items. Galleria at Roseville will be the first Sacramento area regional shopping center to include an adjacent cityscape, called the Promenade, which will feature several upscale restaurants and distinctive two-level retail stores.

"We are excited about the quality and diversity of Galleria at Roseville's lineup of children's and junior's apparel stores," said Ross B. Glickman, president-leasing for Urban Retail Properties Co. "With abercrombie, Anchor Blue, Charlotte Russe, Children's Place and Juxtapose making their Sacramento area debut, Galleria's young customers should be very pleased."

NEW RETAILERS
Several children's apparel stores will make their area debut at the Galleria. Inspired by Abercrombie and Fitch styles for adults, abercrombie will feature casual clothes including jeans and sweaters, board shorts and bathing suits for boys and girls ages seven to fourteen. Children's Place will also open their first Sacramento area store offering mix and match separates and accessories for boys, girls and newborns.

Also new to the market will be several distinctive junior's apparel stores. Among the hottest teen stores in the country, Anchor Blue will offer the latest styles in pants, tops, screen t-shirts and shorts for both young men and women. Charlotte Russe will feature contemporary clothing and accessories designed with the young woman in mind. d.e.m.o. will offer cross cultural sportswear fashions including many name brands. Juxtapose will specialize in hip clothing and accessories for young girls and women while, The Buckle will offer a wide selection of popular unisex clothing targeting 12 to 14 year-olds.

OTHER DISTINCTIVE RETAILERS
Other prominent children's and junior's apparel and specialty retailers include Gap Kids/Baby Gap, which will feature a wide variety of casual clothing from classic khakis and jeans to sweaters and t-shirts. Limited Too will provide the fashion-conscious girl with the coolest apparel and cosmetics, while Express will offer stylish coordinated clothing and accessories for young women. Pacific Sunwear, which began as a little surf shop in Newport Beach, will feature beachwear and casual clothing for teens and young adults. Wet Seal will feature hip, urban wear geared toward the stylish young woman.

Other Galleria specialty stores geared to children of all ages include The Disney Store, which will feature toys, gifts and apparel for children and adults bearing their favorite Disney characters; K B Toys which will offer the latest toys including board and electronic games, dolls and action figures; and Sanrio which will carry their distinctive line of toys and gifts featuring Hello Kitty, My Melody and Tuxedo Sam. From Mozart to Ricky Martin, Camelot will offer music for everyone's taste, while Electronics Boutique, will feature video games and other video accessories.

Galleria at Roseville is owned and developed by Chicago-based Urban Shopping Centers, Inc. (NYSE: URB), a self-administered real estate investment trust (REIT), which is in the business of owning, acquiring, managing, leasing, developing and redeveloping super-regional and regional malls. The company opened Brandon TownCenter in Tampa, Florida in February 1995, Wolfchase Galleria in Memphis, Tennessee in February 1997, a second Tampa-area mall, Citrus Park Town Center, in March 1999, and expects to open Galleria at Roseville on August 25, 2000 and The Streets at Southpoint in Durham, North Carolina in spring 2002. Urban Shopping Centers, Inc. owns interests in several of the premier shopping centers in the United States including Oakbrook Center (Oak Brook, Illinois), Century City Shopping Center & Marketplace (Los Angeles, California), Water Tower Place (Chicago, Illinois), Old Orchard Center (Skokie, Illinois), Copley Place (Boston, Massachusetts),San Francisco Shopping Centre (San Francisco, California), and The Galleria (Houston, Texas), as well as in Urban Retail Properties Co., its property management, leasing and development affiliate. Urban Retail Properties Co. is one of the nation's largest retail property managers, managing more than 59 million square feet of space in 26 states and the District of Columbia.
