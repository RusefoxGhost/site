GALLERIA AT ROSEVILLE ANNOUNCES
HOME FURNISHING STORES
Crate & Barrel, Pottery Barn, Restoration Hardware and Williams-Sonoma highlight first store announcements for Placer County's newest shopping center


ROSEVILLE - Urban Shopping Centers, Inc. announced today that several prominent home furnishing stores will join the 120 store lineup at Galleria at Roseville, the 1.1 million square foot regional shopping center scheduled to open August 25, 2000. Galleria at Roseville, anchored by Nordstrom, Macy's, JCPenney and Sears, will offer shoppers many upscale stores that are new to the Sacramento market.

"Urban prides itself in attracting unique, upscale and exclusive merchants to its shopping centers. Crate & Barrel, Restoration Hardware, Pottery Barn and Williams-Sonoma are coming to Galleria at Roseville," said Ross B. Glickman, president-leasing, Urban Retail Properties Co. "Given that Placer County is a rapidly growing community and an expanding housing market, these home furnishing stores will become a popular destination for those looking to upgrade and enhance their homes."


NEW RETAILERS

Crate & Barrel, one of the most influential and respected housewares retailers in America, will unveil its first Sacramento area store in spring 2001. This 36,628 square foot two-level store - located on the Promenade, the Galleria's outdoor cityscape adjacent to the shopping center - will feature unique cookware, housewares and a distinctive furniture line. The design and quality of the functional and affordable home and kitchen products are inspired by the world travels of its founders, Gordon and Carole Segal. By initiating trends in product design and presentation, Galleria at Roseville's Crate & Barrel store will feature their trademark display method - now widely imitated throughout the industry - of creating a vignette while massing and organizing merchandise to convey a sense of style and usability.

Restoration Hardware will debut in the Sacramento area with an 11,472 square foot store at the Galleria, offering high-quality furniture, lighting, cabinet hardware, interior and exterior fittings, gardenware, fixtures, tools, books and other exceptional merchandise. Restoration Hardware's merchandise signage and displays are written personally by CEO Stephen Gordon and skillfully weave tales from his youth with witty observations of modern life. Restoration Hardware features products that rekindle a shopper's childhood memories like the Gardener's Nail Brush, the Ultimate Dustpan and the German Measuring Beaker.


FAMILIAR NAMES, UNIQUE FEATURES

Pottery Barn, the nation's leading purveyor of casual home furnishings will join the lineup at the Galleria at Roseville with exclusive new components. This 11,061 square foot two-level store will contain a special feature that is new to this area: a full service design studio, where customers become their own interior designers by sketching out their living space and coordinating fabrics, furniture and window treatments. Pottery Barn offers stylish, affordable furniture and home accessories, including distinctive lamps, rugs, draperies and comfortably oversized sofas and chairs available in coordinated colors and designs.

Williams-Sonoma, renowned for specialty kitchen utensils, appliances and cooking products, will open a spacious 5,100 square foot Grande Cuisine store. Grande Cuisine features, such as central "tasting bars" and extensive demonstration kitchens, complement Williams-Sonoma's exceptional selection of cookware, serveware, tools, linens, distinctive foods, cooking ingredients and a comprehensive cookbook selection.


GALLERIA AT ROSEVILLE ANCHORS

Galleria at Roseville will be anchored by four prominent department stores offering their new state-of-the-art facilities and merchandising: Nordstrom with 144,000 square feet, Macy's with 180,000 square feet, JCPenney with 126,338 square feet and Sears with 150,529 square feet. This strong anchor line-up will provide customers with traditional designer apparel, home accessories, furnishings and appliances.

Galleria at Roseville is owned and developed by Chicago-based Urban Shopping Centers, Inc., a self-administered real estate investment trust (REIT), which is in the business of owning, acquiring, managing, leasing, developing and redeveloping super-regional and regional malls. The company opened Brandon TownCenter in Tampa, Florida in February 1995 and Wolfchase Galleria in Memphis, Tennessee in February 1997, a second Tampa-area mall, Citrus Park Town Center, in March 1999, and expects to open Galleria at Roseville on August 25, 2000 and The Streets at Southpoint in Durham, North Carolina in spring 2002. Urban Shopping Centers, Inc. owns interests in several of the premier shopping centers in the United States including Oakbrook Center (Oak Brook, Illinois), Century City Shopping Center & Marketplace (Los Angeles, California), Water Tower Place (Chicago, Illinois), Old Orchard Center (Skokie, Illinois), Copley Place (Boston, Massachusetts), San Francisco Shopping Centre (San Francisco, California), and The Galleria (Houston, Texas), as well as in Urban Retail Properties Co., its property management, leasing and development affiliate. Urban Retail Properties Co. is one of the nation's largest retail property managers, managing more than 60 million square feet of space in 26 states and the District of Columbia.
